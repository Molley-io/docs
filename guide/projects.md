# Working with Projects

Projects are where the real work happens in Molley. Each project represents a product, service, or initiative you're building.

## What is a Project?

A Project in Molley contains:

- **Product information** — Name, description, target audience
- **Research** — Notes, documents, and synthesis
- **Features** — Ideas, Kanban boards, and requirements
- **Growth tools** — Outreach, marketing, and CRM
- **Feedback** — Forms and submissions

## Creating a Project

### Step 1: Start Creation

1. Navigate to your company
2. Click **+ New Project** in the sidebar or project selector
3. The project creation wizard opens

### Step 2: Basic Information

| Field | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Name** | Product or project name | "TaskFlow Pro" |
| **Slug** | URL-friendly identifier | "taskflow-pro" |
| **Description** | What this product does | "A task management app for remote teams" |

### Step 3: Product Details

Complete the product overview to enable better AI assistance:

#### Target Audience
- Who is this product for?
- What problems do they face?
- What are their goals?

#### Value Proposition
- What makes this product unique?
- Why would someone choose it over alternatives?

#### Key Features (Summary)
- List the main capabilities
- This helps AI understand your product scope

::: tip AI Context
When you fill in product details, Molley AI uses this information to:
- Suggest relevant features
- Write appropriate copy
- Understand your constraints
- Provide market-aware recommendations
:::

### Product Section

| Area | Purpose |
|------|---------|

## Project Settings

Access project settings by clicking the **gear icon** or navigating to **Settings**:

### General Settings
- Update name, description, and details
- Change project visibility
- Archive or delete the project

### Integrations
- Connect external tools
- Configure webhooks
- Set up automation

## Working with Multiple Projects

### Switching Projects

1. Click the **Project selector** in the sidebar
2. Choose from your available projects
3. The interface updates to show that project's content

### Project-Specific Context

When you switch projects, Molley AI automatically updates its context. If you're working on "TaskFlow Pro" and ask for feature suggestions, the AI considers:

- TaskFlow Pro's target audience
- Existing features in TaskFlow Pro
- The product's value proposition
- Your company's overall strategy

## Best Practices

### 1. Complete Your Product Profile

The more details you provide, the better AI assistance you'll receive. Take time to fill in:
- Target audience details
- Value proposition
- Key differentiators

### 2. Use Consistent Naming

Choose clear, descriptive names for:
- Projects
- Features
- Requirements

This helps team members and AI understand your work.

### 3. Keep Features Organized

Use the Kanban board to track feature status:
- **Backlog** — Ideas to consider
- **In Consideration** — Being evaluated
- **In Refinement** — Being detailed
- **In Progress** — Currently building
- **Released** — Shipped to users

### 4. Link Related Items

Connect features to:
- Requirements (break down the work)
- Bugs (track issues)
- Research notes (capture context)
